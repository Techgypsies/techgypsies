Kin Lane

Kin Lane is known as the API Evangelist - not in the sense that he evangelizes a single API to developers, but insofar as he works to make everyone API aware. This involves understanding not just the technology, but the business and politics of APIs.

  • Site: kinlane.com

API Evangelist

Application Programming Interfaces (API) are the way business is done on the Internet today. Much like having a website was a pressing question in the 1990s, companies today are scrambling to get their API houses in order, and Kin Lane's API Evangelist site is dedicated to providing analysis that goes beyond just the tech, helping companies understand the importance of the business of their APIs.

  • Site: apievangelist.com

API Voice

Application Programming Interfaces (API) are fast becoming the pipes in which information flows in our increasingly digital economy, delivering the vital resources we need for websites, mobile applications, and Internet connected devices. API Voice, written by Kin Lane, is dedicated to understanding the politics of API design, deployment, management, monetization, and evangelism.

  • Site: apivoice.com

Audrey Watters

Audrey Watters is writer who focuses on education technology - the relationship between politics, pedagogy, business, culture, and ed-tech. Although she was two chapters into her PhD dissertation, she decided to abandon academia, and she now happily fulfills the one job recommended to her by a junior high aptitude test: freelance writer.

  • Site: audreywatters.com

Hack Education

Hack Education chronicles "what's new" in education technology, pointing out how often the hype about "the new" is utterly misguided. (And it's often what's actually quite old.) The site, written by Audrey Watters, offers a weekly round-up of news, as well as ongoing analysis of developments - politically and pedagogically - in the sector.

  • Site: hackeducation.com

Teaching Machines

Audrey Watters' second book, due out in late 2015, examines the cultural history of teaching machines and the long-running drive to automate education. From Sidney Pressey's testing machines of the 1920s, to B F Skinner's teaching machines of the 1950s, and beyond, these machines raise important questions about the future of labor and learning.

  • Site: teachingmachin.es
